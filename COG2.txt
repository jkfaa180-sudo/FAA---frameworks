Duration & sequencing logic (3–4 sentence summary)

Total derived effort is ≈286 hours, which at your 4–5 h/day capacity yields a ~9-week (≈2-month) recommended duration — and per your instruction I'm stating that the leanest responsible scope exceeds the 1-month horizon rather than compressing it unsafely, because GK breadth and zero-background Accountancy are the binding constraints. Sequencing is not syllabus order: it follows the dependency map, anchoring on the Accountancy critical chain as the backbone, running GK + Current Affairs as a daily spaced thread from day one, and slotting Science/Computers/English as high-yield, fatigue-resistant fillers to protect the score floor. Stage 2 — Roadmap System: JKSSB FAA (Finance)

Labels:[OFFICIAL] · [SCIENCE] · [INFERRED]. Tier 1 = standalone exam-ready (Critical+High). Tier 2 = full-mastery extension (Medium+Low). Durations scoped to the lean 1-month intensity assumption applied across a ~9-week scope (per Stage 1 Timeline Justification).

───

A. Two structural decisions that govern every phase

Decision 1 — GK & Current Affairs run as a continuous daily thread, never a phase.[SCIENCE] Spaced repetition and the spacing effect show that high-breadth memory material is retained far better by daily low-dose exposure than by blocked cramming. Blocking 43 hours of GK would also collide with the forgetting curve before exam day. So GK gets ~30–40 min/day every day from Day 1, plus a 15-min current-affairs slot, and is never the "main" topic of a day.

Decision 2 — Accountancy is the spine; everything else is scheduled around its critical chain. Because it's the only strictly sequential subject and the heaviest lift from zero, the calendar's skeleton is the Accountancy progression; lighter high-yield subjects (Science, Computers, English, easy Stats/Eco) are interleaved[SCIENCE] to break cognitive load and exploit the contrast effect, and to give recovery on days when Accountancy is intense.

───

B. Why this order beats syllabus order (major sequencing decisions)

1. Accountancy first and continuous — not "Subject 2."
Syllabus order lists GK first. But Accountancy has the steepest curve and longest dependency chain, so it needs the most calendar runway and the most spaced practice[SCIENCE] (deliberate practice + retrieval over time). Starting it on Day 1 maximizes the number of spaced practice cycles before the exam.
Alternative weighed — weightage-first (GK first, since both are 30): rejected because GK has near-zero prerequisites and can be absorbed late as a thread, whereas Accountancy mastery cannot be rushed at the end. Sequencing by prerequisite depth, not just marks, wins here.

2. Within Accountancy, follow the dependency chain, not the syllabus list. The syllabus lists "Bank Reconciliation" and "Cash Book" before "Ledger" and "Trial Balance," and "Partnership" mid-list. That order breaks prerequisites. Correct teaching order (from Stage 1 Map A): Intro → Equation → Double-Entry/Voucher → Journal → Ledger → Trial Balance → Cash Book → BRS → Final Accounts → Partnership → Cost/Budgetary → factual cluster + Taxation.
Why superior: each topic's output is the next topic's input (journal feeds ledger feeds trial balance feeds final accounts). Teaching out of order forces re-teaching → wasted hours and weaker retention.

3. Math algebra-reactivation is front-loaded but deep-calculus is deferred.[INFERRED] You're rusty-but-recoverable, so a short early reactivation block (linear eqns, sets, functions, interest) unlocks both Math and Statistics (central tendency, probability). Calculus/3D/vectors are pushed late and kept recognition-level because they're the worst hours-per-mark in the paper.
Alternative weighed — teach all Math early in difficulty order: rejected; it would burn high-effort hours on ~3 low-yield marks before securing 60+ easy marks elsewhere.

4. Economics is split micro/macro and interleaved, not blocked. Macro (national income, fiscal/monetary, RBI, reforms) is taught alongside Accountancy's PFMS/taxation and GK's economic-reforms topics. [SCIENCE] Interleaving related-but-distinct material strengthens discrimination and recall, and the overlap means these marks are partly "free."
Alternative weighed — block Economics as one unit: rejected; loses the synergy and the spacing benefit.

5. Science & Computers are distributed as fillers, not a dedicated late phase. They're pure recall with the best score-per-hour, so they're ideal for fatigue days and as interleaved contrast to numerical Accountancy/Math, and they begin early enough to get ≥2 spaced revisions.
Alternative weighed — cram Science+Computers in the final week: rejected; single-exposure cramming of 20 marks risks the forgetting curve right when mock pressure peaks.

C. Phase-wise Roadmap

8 phases. These overlap deliberately (GK thread + revision are continuous), so phase durations sum to more than 9 weeks of "blocks" — they describe streams, not exclusive time. Week mapping is finalized in Stage 3.

Tier labeling convention: Tier 1 content is the standalone exam-ready core. Tier 2 items are explicitly marked [T2]. If the horizon shortens, drop all [T2] and the plan still completes every phase (stress-tested in Section H).

Phase 1 — Foundation (≈ Week 1)
• Objective: Install the core mental models that everything depends on — the accounting equation + double-entry logic, and reactivate algebra. Start the GK/CA daily thread.
• Topics: Accountancy Intro & terms → Accounting Equation → Double-Entry & Voucher approach. Math: algebra reactivation (linear eqns, sets, relations & functions). GK thread begins (J&K History + Geography). Computers fundamentals as warm-up filler.
• Intensity: Moderate (conceptual, low volume). Protect comprehension over speed.
• Methods:[SCIENCE]Feynman (explain the equation in your own words), active recall flashcards for debit/credit rules.
• Deliverables: Debit/credit rule card; 1-page accounting-equation cheat sheet; GK error-log started.
• Exit criteria: Can classify any transaction's effect on the accounting equation and state debit/credit for assets/liabilities/expenses/income without notes, ≥90% on a 20-item self-quiz.
• Tier: Entirely Tier 1.

Phase 2 — Core Learning (≈ Weeks 2–5, the longest stream)
• Objective: Build the full Accountancy chain + secure all high-yield easy subjects.
• Topics (in dependency order): Journal → Ledger → Trial Balance → Cash Book → BRS → Final Accounts (Trading/P&L/Balance Sheet) → Partnership → Cost/Budgetary → factual cluster (Audit, Social Acct/Audit, Cash-based, PFMS, Indian Fin. Mgmt, Developments) → Taxation/GST. Interleaved: Science (all), Computers (OS/MS Office/Internet), English (grammar mechanics), Statistics easy half (Data, Central Tendency, Index Numbers, Demography), Economics (Macro first, then Micro), Math (Interest, Probability, then [T2] P&C/Binomial/Matrices).
• Intensity: High. This is the workload peak.
• Methods:[SCIENCE]Deliberate practice (graded Accountancy problem sets), interleaving (alternate numerical Accountancy with recall-based Science/Computers within a day), retrieval practice (start-of-day cold quiz on prior day), error analysis (maintain per-subject error log).
• Deliverables: Worked-problem notebook for each Accountancy topic; formula sheets (Stats/Math/Interest); grammar-rules sheet.
• Exit criteria: Independently prepare a full set of final accounts from a trial balance with 2 adjustments; ≥75% on sectional quizzes for Science, Computers, English, easy Stats.
• Tier: Tier 1 = Accountancy chain through Final Accounts + Partnership + Science + Computers + English + Eco Macro + Stats easy + Math (Interest/Probability). Tier 2 = Eco Micro depth, Stats Attributes, Math P&C/Binomial/Matrices.

Phase 3 — Consolidation (≈ Week 6, overlapping end of Phase 2)
• Objective: Close remaining Tier 1 gaps; teach deferred hard/low-yield topics to recognition level.
• Topics: Taxation/GST + Accountancy factual cluster finalized. [T2] Math Limits & Derivatives, Coordinate/3D Geometry, Vectors — recognition-level only. Economics Micro completed.

• Continuous from Day 1, not a late phase. Daily 20–30 min start-of-day cold retrieval of prior 1–3 days.

E. Practice Strategy [SCIENCE]
• Progressive difficulty: within each Accountancy topic, basic → moderate → exam-level; never advanced application before the prerequisite is taught (enforced by dependency order).

• Error analysis loop: every wrong answer categorized (concept / silly / guess) → concept errors feed revision, silly errors feed checklist, guesses feed the skip rule.
