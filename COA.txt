4. Recommended Preparation Duration + Timeline Justification

• Total derived workload: ≈ 245 hours [INF] (bottom-up from the table; Stage 4 will reconcile against Stage 3's daily sum).
• Sustainable capacity: 4.5 hrs/day average (midpoint of your 4–5 hr range; using midpoint avoids planning at the ceiling, which protects against burnout — [LS] spacing/sustainability).
• 245 ÷ 4.5 ≈ 54.4 study-days. Adding 1 rest/buffer day per 6 study days (sustainability + spaced consolidation, [LS]) → 54.4 × (7/6) ≈ 63.5 calendar days.

→ Recommended duration: ≈ 9 weeks (≈ 63 calendar days), even at the leanest responsible scoping.

Timeline Justification (required, per the Exam Timeline Assumption): The 1-month (≈30-day) working horizon is not achievable for genuine readiness for a true zero-background beginner, because Accountancy alone (~90 hrs, and internally sequential so it can't be parallelized away) consumes ~20 study-days by itself, and it is 25% of the marks plus a prerequisite for nothing else being safely guessable under negative marking. Compressing to 30 days would force either (a) skipping ~half of Accountancy — sacrificing the joint-highest-weight subject — or (b) >7 hr/day, violating the sustainability constraint. Per the rule, I state this explicitly rather than compress unsafely: the leanest responsible plan is ~9 weeks. If the exam is confirmed sooner, the Tier-1-only subset below (~150–160 hrs ≈ 5 weeks) is the fallback that still forms a coherent exam-ready plan — that is the lever, not unsafe compression of the full plan.

A. Master Sequencing Logic — why dependency order beats syllabus order

The syllabus lists subjects 1–8 and lists Accountancy topics in a non-teachable order (e.g., it places "Bank Reconciliation" and "Cash Book" before "Ledger" and "Double Entry" — pedagogically backwards). I therefore sequence by the Dependency Map (Stage 1 §2) , not syllabus order.

Three candidate orderings weighed [INF]:

1. Strict syllabus order — rejected: teaches BRS before Ledger/Double Entry, violating prerequisites; would force re-teaching and inflate hours. Fails QC item 2 and 8.
2. Strict weightage-first order (do all of GK-30 + Accountancy-30 first, then the 10s) — partially attractive because it front-loads marks. Rejected as the primary axis because: (a) GK is shallow and decays → front-loading it wastes the retention window ([LS] spacing favors distributing memorization, not massing it); (b) it ignores that Accountancy is internally sequential and cognitively heavy — doing 90 hrs of hard accounting in one unbroken block spikes cognitive load and crushes a beginner ([LS] cognitive-load + interleaving). Weightage still governs hour totals (Stage 1), just not the day-to-day sequence.
3. Dependency-driven, interleaved, weightage-weighted (chosen) — Accountancy's linear chain runs as the continuous spine across the whole plan at ~40–50% of daily load; the non-sequential subjects (GK, Science, Computers, English, Economics) are interleaved as daily second/third blocks to (a) give the brain spacing between hard accounting sessions ([LS] interleaving improves discrimination + retention), (b) distribute memorization subjects across the timeline so spaced repetition works, and (c) keep daily load sustainable. Maths/Statistics' hard topics are deferred and capped (low ROI under negative marking). This satisfies all QC items.

One nuance kept from weightage-first: within the interleaved subjects, higher-mark/high-ROI material (J&K GK, Computers, English grammar, Eco macro) is scheduled earlier and revisited more, while low-ROI Maths/Stats tails come late and light. So weightage shapes priority and revisit frequency within the interleave, even though dependency shapes the spine.

B. Phase-Wise Roadmap

Eight phases, scoped to the leanest-sufficient 9-week (~63-day) plan. Durations in study-days (buffer days excluded; they're distributed ~1 per 6). Revision is continuous from Phase 2 onward (not bolted on). Mocks increase toward the end.

Reading the phases: Phases overlap within a day (spine + interleave), but progress linearly as labeled. "Exit criteria" are competency gates — the next phase's prerequisites.

───

PHASE 0 — Foundation & Onboarding
• Objective: Install the accounting "mental model" before any mechanics; set up the error log + spaced-repetition system. [LS] A schema-first start prevents rote-without-understanding.
• Duration: 2 study-days.
• Topics: Accountancy: Intro to Financial Accounting & terms, Accounting Equation(T1) . Setup: error log, flashcard deck (Anki/paper), how active recall + Feynman will be used.
• Intensity: Light–moderate (deliberately gentle ramp).
• Methods:Feynman (explain "what is accounting" in own words), Active Recall (terms).
• Deliverables: Accounting-equation worksheet solved; 30 starter flashcards; error log created.
• Exit criteria: Can state Assets = Liabilities + Capital and classify 15/15 sample items correctly; can define the 12 core terms unaided.

───

PHASE 1 — Core Learning (the big build)
• Objective: Teach every Tier-1 spine concept and the high-ROI interleave subjects to first-pass mastery. This is the largest phase.
• Duration: ~30 study-days (the bulk of the 245-hr workload).
• Spine (T1, sequential): Double Entry → Journal/Voucher → Ledger → Trial Balance → Cash Book → BRS → Final Accounts (Trading/P&L/Balance Sheet) → Partnership.
• Interleaved alongside (daily 2nd/3rd blocks):
◦ T1: J&K UT GK; Computers (all); English grammar core; Indian Geography/Culture/Environment GK; Eco intro+macro; Maths easy set (SI/CI, Linear eqns, Set theory, Relations & Functions); Science Bio/Chem/Environment.
◦ T2 (later in phase): Eco micro; Science physics; Stats data+central tendency.
• Intensity: Moderate–high but capped at 5 hrs/day; hardest accounting topics scheduled on freshest (early-day) blocks.
• Methods:Deliberate Practice (accounting drills), Interleaving (spine + 2 unrelated subjects/day), Retrieval Practice (daily start = recall yesterday), Progressive Difficulty (simple journal entries → adjustments).
• Deliverables: Worked-problem sets per topic; running error log; flashcards for every memorization subject.
• Exit criteria: Can prepare a full set of Final Accounts with 3+ adjustments unaided; ≥70% on untimed topic quizzes for all T1 spine topics; J&K GK + Computers first-pass complete.

───

PHASE 2 — Consolidation & Continuous First Revision (runs overlapping the tail of Phase 1)
• Objective: Convert first-pass knowledge into durable, retrievable knowledge; close the remaining T2 topics.
• Duration: ~8 study-days (partly woven into Phase 1's last week — revision is not deferred).
• Topics: Spaced revision of all Phase 0–1 material; finish remaining T2: Accountancy govt-cluster (Cost/Budget, Taxation, Financial Mgmt/Audit, Social Accounting/Audit, PFMS, Indian FMS, Developments, Cash-based system); English vocab+comprehension; Maths Matrices; Stats Index/CPI/WPI + Demography; Low-tier Maths/Stats (P&C/Binomial/Probability, Limits/Derivatives, Coord/3D/Vectors, Attributes) at minimal selective depth.
• Intensity: Moderate; shift from learning-new toward recall.
• Methods:Spaced Repetition (1-day/3-day/7-day flashcard intervals), Active Recall, Feynman for weak concepts, Error Analysis (categorize error-log entries).
• Deliverables: First full revision sweep done; govt-cluster + low-tier topics covered to target depth.
• Exit criteria: Every syllabus topic touched at least once; flashcard deck stable; error log categorized by error type.

───

PHASE 3 — PYQ-Based Application
• Objective: Map learning onto the actual question style and difficulty; identify real weak areas via evidence, not feeling.
• Duration: ~5 study-days.
• Topics: Previous-year questions (JKSSB FAA + analogous JKSSB/SSC accounts-assistant papers) topic-by-topic, then mixed.
• Intensity: High cognitive, moderate volume.
• Methods:Retrieval Practice under near-exam conditions; Error Analysis on every wrong/guessed item; Interleaving (mixed-topic PYQ sets).
• Deliverables: PYQ accuracy log per subject; ranked weak-topic list.
• Exit criteria: ≥2 full PYQ-style topic sweeps; weak list produced for Phase 5.

───

PHASE 4 — Mock Tests (escalating cadence)
• Objective: Build exam stamina, timing, and a negative-marking decision rule.
• Duration: ~5 study-days (interleaved with Phase 5).
• Topics: Full 120-Q / 2-hr mocks.
• Intensity: High; full-length simulation.
• Methods:Deliberate Practice on pacing; Error Analysis post-mock; negative-marking strategy (see §E).
• Deliverables: ≥3 full mocks with post-mortems.
• Exit criteria: Stable/ improving score across mocks; finishing within 2 hrs; guessing rule internalized.

───

PHASE 5 — Weak-Area Improvement (interleaved with Phase 4)
• Objective: Targeted repair of evidence-based weak topics.
• Duration: ~3 study-days (folded into Phases 4 & 6).
• Methods:Deliberate Practice narrowed to weak topics; Feynman re-teach; Error Analysis loop.
• Exit criteria: Each previously-weak topic reaches ≥70% on a fresh quiz.

───

PHASE 6 — Final Revision

• Negative-marking decision rule (from your 0.25 confirmation [OFFICIAL]):

◦ Skip only if it's a pure 4-way blind guess in a Low-tier topic and you're already time-pressured — pure random is barely +EV (+0.0625), so default is attempt with elimination, skip is the exception.
